So What's The Solution?

It's a hard pill to swallow. So here it is... In terms of living within the bounds of what the planet requires, and to live comfortablly while also feeling like it's the 21st century, the solution is:

Society needs to gravitate toward living in a "Cybertruck style" tiny home, towable, transportable, and capable of producing and managing it's own fresh water, food, electricity, hygiene for occupents, and to have everything be reliable, repairable by onboard occupents, and safe for at least an onboard family or group of at least five adult people.

From an engineering perspective, this is an utterly insane ask. It's like trying to land on Mars, profitably.

"Off-grid", "decentralization", these are the terms of a nerdy, self-proficent folk who want to live in the middle of nowhere, right? Well these are the claims of a society that has always known centralized systems, interdependant on eachother. We have the technology today, for implementing self proficent systems to allow anyone to live practically anywhere in the world, and yet still feel incredibly connected to the rest of society.

So why isn't this idea more mainstream? The answer is simple, we have old systems that still exist because corporations stand to benefit from keeping the system in place. It's akin to the disruption that Tesla caused on the automotive and energy industries. Building out technology to improve self-reliance is a movement that creates a lot of powerful enemies. Tesla I believe, is not the example, but rather the exception to this rule.

Even with this in mind, companies like Tesla and others will come and go, ultimately building products that are designed to be limited in access to just a subset of the wealthiest of humanity. So what about the rest of us? How do we have access to off-grid technologies without paying a premium? That's where we come in. We're open source, fully transparent, and fully commited to creating a self-reliant society. We don't require funding or investment, that's not why we're here. We're here to grow the cause of off-grid technologies. To be accessable to as many people as possible.

So how do we solve this reality that the world is heading towards ever higher risk of collapse?

Welcome to the greatest century of innovation. The rate at which technology progresses boggles the mind. Everyday the rate is accelerating, faster and faster. We shoot for the stars (understandably), whilst our people's rate of migration over the next thirty years alone will be more than 200 million people. The America's and European political upheaval movements caused by the Syrian Refugee Crisis is estimated to have caused the mass migration of around 6.8 million people. This estimate by 2050 would be the outcome of not just a dozen Syrian Refugee level events, but over 30 within the next 30 years.

Events following in parallel with the Syrian Refugee Crisis total at least 24.7 million migrants starting with the Bolivarian Revolution in 2014 to the 2022 Russian Invasion of Ukraine. This coupled with Syria's total means 31.5 million people have migrated from new events since 2011. That gives an average of at least 3 million migrants per year from regime aggression alone. Although this is about half the rate which was alluded to in the previous estimate, this is already the current rate. As drought and famine increase, authoritarian regimes will rise from this societal degradation. Leading to an ever higher rate of migration.

Just by looking at this simple fact alone, you must ask yourself; if I had to migrate to a different country/state right now, based on an unforeseen calamity, what would I have to leave? When food, supplies, and money go low, what will you do? At some point, it boils down to three things: food, water, and shelter. Without these, we're fracked!
